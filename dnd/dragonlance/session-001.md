# Session 1: The Funeral and the First Blood

The adventure began with a somber invitation. The party received a letter from Becklin Uth Vaharin bearing the tragic news that your mutually cherished friend, the legendary adventurer Ispin Greenshield, had passed away. The letter cordially requested your presence at his upcoming funeral service in the peaceful village of Vogler. After converging and meeting up with the rest of your companions in the bustling city of Kalaman, you set out together along the highway toward Vogler. As you traveled, the air grew heavy with unsettling rumors from passing merchants whispering of an encroaching war in the east.

The gravity of these rumors manifested quickly. Along the road, a panicked young man came speeding toward you on horseback, shouting a frantic warning that the knights who had hired him as a guide had just been brutally ambushed by terrifying, reptilian-looking creatures just a short distance ahead. Springing into action, the party rushed forward to the scene of the attack. You engaged the strange monsters, successfully slaying two of the reptilian beasts while a trio of them managed to scatter and escape into the brush. With the immediate threat neutralized, you gathered yourselves and pressed onward to Vogler.

Upon arriving, you found a vibrant settlement preparing for a festival. You spent the afternoon exploring the town's local landmarks, pausing to visit the bustling docks of the Fishbowl and the welcoming atmosphere of the Brass Crab tavern. Your curiosity eventually led you to a sprawling mercenary camp pitched just north of the village. There you learned that these mercenaries, under the strict leadership of a commander named Cudgel, were scheduled to participate in a grand grand re-enactment of the historical Battle of the High Hill the following afternoon. During your tour, you were formally introduced to Becklin Uth Vaharin, Cudgel Ironclad, and several other local figures.

As twilight painted the river, the community gathered to send off Ispin Greenshield. Together with Becklin and Cudgel, you sent his funeral vessel down the water, transitioning into a lively reception filled with drinking and the sharing of Ispin's many legendary tall tales. The celebration was briefly disrupted by a pompous noble named Lord Bakaris, who loudly heckled the presenters, yawning dramatically and insulting the quality of the stories. Ignoring the noble's arrogance, Becklin pulled you aside to request that you pass by Thornwall Keep the next morning, revealing that Ispin had left a specific parting gift for the group. Over breakfast the following day, her squire, Barrett, presented you with Ispin’s beautiful, signature green shield.

By noon, the Mayor officially kicked off the village festival, and the party eagerly signed up for the local fishing competition. To the absolute astonishment of the crowd, Dave successfully reeled in the fabled "Benebog the Line Breaker," instantly cementing his status as a local celebrity throughout Vogler. Naturally, the miserable Lord Bakaris was present once again, doing his absolute best to sour the festive mood.

Shortly after the tournament concluded, the celebratory parade marched out toward High Hill to begin the grand re-enactment. The local town militia donned costumes to play the noble Knights of Solamnia, while Cudgel's mercenaries took up the mantle of the wicked forces of Istar. While both sides were strictly ordered to utilize blunt wooden props, the atmosphere shifted instantly as the lines clashed; the mercenaries were unmistakably wielding sharpened, lethal steel. A terrifyingly real battle erupted on the field. The party fought valiarly amidst the chaos, cutting through a contingent of treacherous mercenaries and subduing their ringleader, a half-orc named Gregaris. It quickly became clear that Gregaris had secretly bribed a massive portion of the mercenary company behind Cudgel's back. In the aftermath of the betrayal, the spectators were left utterly paralyzed with shock—most notably Cudgel, Mayor Raven, and Becklin. Deeply grateful for your swift intervention, the town council promised a handsome reward for saving the militia from a total massacre.
