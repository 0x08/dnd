# Session 7: The Blood of Steel Springs

Following the total liberation of the fortress, Commander Cudgel officially established Wheelwatch Outpost as her new base of operations. She provided the party with an official military dispatch to deliver back to Marshal Vendri in Kalaman. Before departing, you descended to the dungeons to free the captives, identifying them as Lanal Brint and Elgo Duckditcher, kindly returning a stolen hoopak weapon to her possession. You then conducted a brief interrogation of your captured soldier; after extracting a few fragmented pieces of intelligence regarding the Dragon Army's movements, the party swiftly executed him to ensure no leaks.

Your return journey to the city of Kalaman was entirely uneventful. However, upon entering the command castle, you were alarmed to discover that Marshal Vendri had unexpectedly marched out to the far west, taking over half of the city's active military force with her. The remaining garrison had been hastily deployed to strike down a rogue enemy contingent that had broken away from the main host. Shockingly, this secondary force was being led by the incompetent Lord Bakaris and his arrogant son, with your liaison officer Darrett riding alongside them.

The battle plan was to ambush the Red Dragon Army near a strategic river crossing of the Inkwater river located thirty miles west of Kalaman, a site known locally as Steel Springs. Finding a frantic, handwritten tactical note left behind by Darrett, the party immediately mobilized, racing across the countryside toward the coordinates.

Upon arriving at Steel Springs, you were thrown into a chaotic, raging battlefield. The party plunged into the fray, engaging a disciplined troop of mounted cavalry soldiers backed by a terrifying, flying reptilian beast known as a Dragonnel. A brutal, muddy skirmish ensued, forcing the enemy commander into a desperate tactical retreat. Following the retreating lines for six miles east of Steel Springs, you finally located Darrett’s hidden command encampment.

Safe within the trenches, Darrett explained the full tactical reality of the situation, confirming that Bakaris's arrogance had severely jeopardized the operation. After a long night's rest in the camp, Darrett encouraged the characters to immediately ride back to Kalaman to brief Governor Miat on the shifting front lines.

The party sped back to the city, hailing the guards to have the heavy portcullis raised. Wishing to remain useful, Dave temporarily left the main group to assist the municipal engineers in constructing a massive defensive wall to protect the incoming waves of frontier evacuees. The remaining party members marched into the castle, only to find their path barred by a pair of towering, ancient knights guarding the heavy doors to the council chamber. Refusing to tolerate the delay, Blip aggressively attempted to shove past the guards, instantly triggering a curse as the armor rattled to life—and we rolled initiative!
