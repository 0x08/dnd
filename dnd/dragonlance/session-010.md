# Session 10: Into the Northern Wastes

Following your harrowing expedition through the subterranean catacombs, Darrett formally approached the party to invite you to an emergency war council with Marshal Vendri. Before attending the high-stakes meeting, however, the exhausted taskforce secured a comfortable, uninterrupted night's rest to fully recover their strength. During the quiet evening hours, Boycott sat in deep meditation, successfully attuning to the magical Kagonesti Forest Shroud you had recovered from the crypts.

The next morning, you marched into the war room to meet with Marshal Vendri. While the battle-hardened Marshal was initially highly unenthusiastic about chasing legends, Darrett delivered a brilliant tactical argument that successfully convinced her that the remote Northern Wastes required an immediate, heavy military investigation. To prepare for the expedition, the party spent the afternoon researching within the castle's grand library, successfully uncovering a rare historical text titled *The Rough Guide to the Northern Wastes* along with a detailed geographical map of the uncharted region.

During your research, the arrogant Lord Bakaris began causing trouble once again, attempting to disrupt your preparations. To neutralize the nuisance, the party paid a band of local street urchins twenty-five gold pieces to aggressively terrorize and keep a watchful eye on the noble for the next seven days. To maximize the psychological warfare, you even provided the kids with a massive glass jar filled entirely with Marmaduke’s pungent cat urine to utilize during their pranks. Before departing, you dropped off your legendary Dragonlance at the Twin Hammerstrike Forges for extensive repairs, with the blacksmiths promising it would be fully restored in exactly one week.

With assignments in hand, Marshal Vendri officially deployed the characters to the Northern Wastes to uncover the Dragon Army’s ultimate agenda. The party set sail from Kalaman harbor aboard a heavy military transport, accompanied by both Cudgel and Tatina Rookledust, who had proudly volunteered to join the dangerous voyage.

The fleet eventually dropped anchor near a treacherous coastal region known as Wrecker's Edge. The party loaded into a small rowboat to scout the coastline for a secure beachhead to land the larger troop ships. During the scouting run, you spotted an abandoned sailing vessel drifting in the mist and boarded it to investigate. Unfortunately, you accidentally triggered a magical pressure trap on the deck, causing two roaring Air Elementals to materialize and attack. The party fought through the wind vortexes, dispersing the elementals.

Suddenly, a mysterious elven mage named Dalamar materialized on the deck. He introduced himself and openly shared his reasons for exploring the wastes, politely extending an exclusive invitation for the party to visit his hidden elven encampment. With the coast clear, the military ships successfully landed, and the Kalaman army established a heavily fortified base camp at Wrecker's Edge, intending to march toward the elven camp at first light.

The next morning, as you journeyed through the scarred terrain toward the coordinates, your path was blocked by a patrol of Dragon Army soldiers backed by a fierce Draconian officer. The party responded instantly, deploying a barrage of devastating *fireball* spells that rapidly incinerated the enemies.

Upon entering the elven camp, you were greeted coldly by their leader, Zhelsuel, who was completely unhappy to see human military forces invading their sacred lands. Frustrated by his leader's stubborn isolationism, Dalamar pulled the party aside and made a secret, mutually beneficial deal: he promised to share all of his hard-earned historical research in exchange for the party's tactical assistance in the field. Dalamar stepped up to your war map and marked three precise archaeological locations that he believed held the key to the region's magic: the Blue Phoenix Shrine, the Sunward Fortress, and the obelisk of Wakenreth. Eager to begin, the party set off toward the Blue Phoenix Shrine, with Dalamar riding alongside you as a guide.
