# Session 16: The Raid on Camp Carrionclay

Following the total eradication of the Draconian forces within Bluemaw Cave, the party formally concluded their contract with Grasha, bidding the dwarves farewell as you loaded your gear back into the supply carts. Your next objective was clear: you must march on the fortified enemy outpost of Camp Carrionclay to liberate the captured Sea Elves.

However, as your wagon train crossed a massive alkali flat, the desert floor exploded once again as *another* titanic Purple Worm surged from the depths to attack. A brutal, high-stakes battle erupted in the sand dunes. In the absolute heat of the melee, a bizarre magical anomaly occurred, causing Snub to suddenly and unexpectedly transform into a powerful beast form. Through sheer martial violence, the party ultimately brought the massive worm crashing down. Searching the nearby terrain after the battle, you uncovered a pair of highly mysterious alchemical potions buried in the sand.

Resuming your march, the party finally arrived at the rocky ridges overlooking the heavily fortified perimeter of Camp Carrionclay. Seeking a tactical advantage, Snub drank another *potion of clairvoyance*, gaining a perfect, detailed layout of the entire enemy garrison, sentry rotations, and prison cages.

To breach the heavily guarded compound, the party devised a chaotic tactical diversion. Slipping through the shadows under the cover of dusk, you systematically set fire to a dozen storage tents along the western wall, sending a roaring inferno into the sky and throwing the garrison into absolute panic. Amidst the spreading smoke and confusion, Dave executed a incredibly cunning, high-stakes ruse: he walked directly up to the front gate and hand-delivered Blip over to the guards as a "captured prisoner." Once Blip was successfully escorted past the throat of the defenses, the trap was sprung. The party launched a total, uncompromising assault from the smoke, cutting off all avenues of escape and systematically wiping out the entire enemy camp in a masterclass of military violence.
