# Session 2: The Cragmaw Hideout

The party cautiously crossed the threshold into the damp caverns of the Cragmaw Hideout, immediately running into a pack of snarling, half-starved wolves chained near the entrance. Seeking to use their captive as a meat shield, the party attempted to aggressively push Grom further down the narrow passage to trigger any hidden traps. However, the clever goblin seized the moment and shrieked at the top of his lungs, successfully alerting the interior sentries. Reacting instantly, the goblins pulled a set of wooden levers, unleashing a massive, roaring wall of dammed water down the tunnel that violently washed the party out of the cave mouth.

Undeterred by the muddy setback, you gathered yourselves, drew your weapons, and marched right back into the cavern. Pushing into a smoke-filled side chamber, you caught a large squad of goblins completely off-guard while they were distracted eating their rations. The party launched a total assault, slaughtering the raiders to a man. Moving deeper into the complex, you confronted a goblin sub-boss named Yeemik; you swiftly executed him and rescued Sildar Hallwinter, who was being held hostage on a high ledge. To save Sildar from the indignity of traveling naked, Kevin quickly tailored a remarkably creative, makeshift suit out of salvaged goblin garments. Armed with a looted scimitar, a grateful Sildar officially joined your ranks.

The party then crossed a natural stone bridge to infiltrate the twin pool cavern, cutting through successive waves of goblin archers. As you entered the final cavern, Ulvor was completely blindsided by the massive bugbear chieftain, Klarg, and nearly perished under the brute's heavy morningstar. However, the party rallied instantly: Klarg’s vicious pet wolf, Ripper, was rapidly put down by the frontline fighters, followed immediately by Wilbin unleashing a perfectly placed *acid dart* that one-shot Klarg, melting the bugbear chieftain where he stood. Searching the cave's storage crates, you discovered a mountain of stolen merchant supplies stamped with a prominent Blue Lion sigil. Prying open one of the reinforced chests, the party discovered and claimed three magnificent suits of pristine chain mail armor.
