# Curse of Strahd: Campaign Lore and Reference

## The Von Zarovich Lineage

The history of Barovia is deeply tied to the bloodline of King Barov and Queen Ravenovia. Their son, Sergei, was tragically murdered by his own brother, Strahd. This dark fratricide was driven by Strahd's obsession with Tatyana, Sergei’s betrothed. Desperate to possess her, Strahd made a horrific pact with the Dark Powers, becoming a vampire. Rather than surrender to Strahd, Tatyana threw herself to her death. Today, Barovia exists as a grim demiplane forged entirely within Strahd's twisted consciousness, trapping him in a Domain of Dread alongside any unfortunate souls who cross the borders.

Strahd believes his soul is utterly lost to evil, harboring no pity or remorse. The only event that truly haunts his hollow existence is the death of Tatyana. Because no humanity remains within him, he relentlessly hunts Ireena Kolyana—the daughter of the late burgomaster of Barovia—believing her to be the latest vessel for Tatyana’s reincarnated soul. Ireena herself is torn, entirely unsure of her destiny and caught between a strange attraction to the vampire lord and a fierce desire to protect her family.

## The Land and its Inhabitants

Barovia is a insular and bleak domain holding fewer than three thousand residents. Finding a suitable mate is a constant struggle for the locals, and the oppressive clouds overhead cast a stark, white, and perpetual twilight across the landscape. The Barovians have developed bizarre customs for dealing with outsiders; they often stare in eerie silence, yet they harbor violent tempers that can flare up at a moment's notice. Should a native be mistreated by an outsider, a intense sense of social cohesion unifies the community against the threat.

Strahd rules this land with absolute authority and absolute madness. When looking out from his castle spires, his angular, powerful face contorts with a tragedy darker than the night itself. He watches the arrival of new adventurers with a twisted smile, seeing them as mere playthings. He tests them with brutal encounters to see if any are worthy of becoming a consort or successor, seeking entertainment by driving wedges between allies. Meanwhile, the legendary vampire hunter Rudolph van Richten operates in the shadows, waiting for his moment to strike.

## The Tarokka Card Readings

During a fateful encounter with Madam Eva, five cards were drawn to illuminate the path toward defeating the darkness:

- **Card 1 (The Hooded One / 7 of Swords):** This card speaks of history, revealing a faceless god waiting at the end of a long, winding mountain road. It indicates that the Tome of Strahd is hidden deep within the Amber Temple.
- **Card 2 (The Abjurer / 4 of Stars):** Symbolizing great hope, protection, and a powerful force for good, this card points toward a fallen house guarded by a great stone dragon. The Holy Symbol of Ravenkind can be found at the highest peak of Argynvostholt.
- **Card 3 (The Druid / 5 of Glyphs):** A card of raw power and strength, it foretells of a weapon of vengeance: a sword of sunlight. The Sunsword lies at the base of the Gulthias tree atop a hill of graves on Yester Hill, where the ancient dead sleep and the ravens watch.
- **Card 4 (The Ghost):** Shedding light on a powerful ally in the battle against the darkness, this card reveals a fallen paladin lingering like a phantom in a dead dragon's lair. Sir Klutz, a phantom warrior residing in Castle Ravenloft, will stand with the party.
- **Card 5 (The Marionette):** Leading directly to the enemy, this card urges the adventurers to look to great heights. Strahd von Zarovich waits near the beating heart of Castle Ravenloft, atop the peak of the north tower.
